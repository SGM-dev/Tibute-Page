Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee

The man who invented the World Wide Web (WWW)

The Story

  • 1955 - Born in London, England
  • 1969 to 1973 - Attended south-west London's Emanuel School
  • 1973 to 1976 - Studied Bachelor of Arts in Physics
  • 1976 to 1978 - Worked as an engineer at the telecommunications company Plessary
  • 1978 - Joined D. G. Nash in Ferndown, Dorset, where he helped create typesetting software for printers
  • June to December 1980 - worked as an independent contractor at CERN from June to December 1980 where he proposed a project based on the concept of hypertext, to facilitate sharing and updating information among researchers, demonstrating it with a prototype named ENQUIRE
  • late 1980 to 1983 - Ran technical side at John Poole's Image Computer Systems, Ltd, in Bournemouth, Dorset working on a "real-time remote procedure call" which gave him experience in computer networking
  • 1984 - Returned to CERN as a fellow
  • 1989 - Wrote a proposal to join hypertext with the Internet and joined hands with Robert Cailliau who proposed a similar idea.
  • 1990 - Published the first web site which described the project itself, available to the Internet from the CERN network
  • 1994 - Founded the W3C at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology comprising of various companies that were willing to create standards and recommendations to improve the quality of the Web

"Creating the web was really an act of desperation, because the situation without it was very difficult when I was working at CERN later. Most of the technology involved in the web, like the hypertext, like the Internet, multi-font text objects, had all been designed already. I just had to put them together. It was a step of generalizing, going to a higher level of abstraction, thinking about all the documentation systems out there as being possibly part of a larger imaginary documentation system."

-- Tim Berners-Lee
